UMN Advanced Remote Sensing - Forest Fire Regrowth

My final project for advanced remote sensing. I really enjoyed this project, and I think it illustrates what I would like to do in the future. Find a question, pursure the answer to it, and share the results in a comprehensive way. For this, I downloaded Landsat data and calculated average NDVI for fires from 1997-2020. Past fires were used to estimate the current rate of regrowth for the Carr fire (2018). If I could do it over I would inculde more fires, years, and automate workflows.

Lake Tahoe Basin Management Unit - 2023 Motor Vehicle Use Map (MVUM)

This was the the first priority in my intership with the Forest Service. MVUMs represent roads and motorized trails for the public to use. It is important that they are accurate and updated for the safety of guest and employees. My role in this map was to meet biweekly with the road and the trail coordinators for the Tahoe Basin and make the spatial edits that they request. Imagery and Lidar data were also used for reference, as well as internal Forest Service tools for quaility assurance.

Eldorado National Forest - 2023 Contract Planting Map

This is a fairly standard contract map for the Forest Service. For this map, I was given raw data for the outline of the units. I coordinated with the Timber team to digitize, clean up, and then classify units. The map is used for informing prosective contractors of size and location of units, and if they choose to take the contract, the map would be used in Avenza to determine boundaries.

Eldorado National Forest - School Permit Map

This map demonstrates the location of elementary school buildings on Forest Service land.

Lake Tahoe Basin Management Unit - Caldor Hazard Tree Removal

This is a fairly standard series of contract maps that I would create for the Tahoe Basin. These maps are also used to inform contractors of the size and location of units. Because preserving Lake Tahoe for tourism is so essential, there are extra protections that lead to more intricate maps. These were a data-driven pages series of 11 maps with varying scale and symbology for the removal of hazardous trees. The difficulty I faced here was creating a map where all the features were accurate, visible, and still keep the features dynamic enough to be data-driven.
